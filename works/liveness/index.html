Liveness

Daoism speaks of a quiet kinship among all things, a hidden thread binding the human spirit to the living world. We are not apart from nature, but woven into its fabric—breathing with it, moving as it moves. The same unseen current that stirs the leaves, courses through rivers, and shapes the contours of the land also flows within us. It is a vital force that animates all existence, present in every being and every form, felt more than seen. This is the pulse of the world, what we might call “liveness.”
